## Executive Summary
This landing page is a warm, editorial, conversion-focused single-page experience for a closed community of 40 people. The message is simple: the user is not buying a course, but entering a small, supportive environment with a buddy system, weekly calls, and a closed chat. The visual language is quiet, premium, terracotta-accented, and deliberately spacious on desktop while becoming simpler, denser, and more readable on mobile.

The page is built as a long narrative with strong emotional framing first, then social proof, then problem agitation, then reframing, then product explanation, then trust and pricing, then FAQ, and finally a strong CTA and legal footer.

## Styling System

### Visual direction
- Warm editorial palette
- Off-white surface background
- Terracotta primary accent
- Quiet ink/charcoal typography
- Soft beige secondary surfaces
- Generous whitespace on desktop
- Tighter, cleaner hierarchy on mobile

### Typography
- Headlines: serif, expressive, strong, slightly editorial
- Body: sans-serif, readable, light enough for comfortable scanning
- Meta / labels: mono, small, uppercase, tracked

### Motion
- Framer Motion is used for fade-ins, staggered headline reveal, the hero counter motif, FAQ expansion, and CTA morphing.
- Motion should remain supportive, not decorative.
- Reduced-motion users should still get a clean static layout.

### Layout rhythm
- The page should move from hook to problem to solution to proof to pricing to FAQ to conversion.
- Mobile must use shorter vertical spacing than desktop.
- The page should avoid dense visual noise, especially early in the funnel.

### Token policy
- All colors, spacing, radius, shadows, motion, and z-index values should come from `artifacts/landing/src/tokens.css`.
- CSS should consume semantic variables, not raw values.
- The audit script should be run before commit.

## Responsive Rules

### Mobile
- Hero copy must be shorter, cleaner, and easier to scan.
- Counter motifs are hidden or simplified where they add noise.
- Cards should use smaller padding and lower visual height.
- Sections should be less tall and more compact.
- CTA touch targets must remain wide and easy to hit.

### Tablet
- Maintain two-column patterns where available.
- Avoid over-stacking if content can still breathe in columns.

### Desktop
- Preserve the editorial look with larger type, larger gaps, and more layered composition.

## Accessibility Rules
- Keep text contrast high enough for the warm palette.
- Ensure CTA and accordion controls are keyboard reachable.
- Use `aria-expanded` on FAQ buttons.
- Motion must not be required to understand the page.
- Tap targets should remain large enough on mobile.
